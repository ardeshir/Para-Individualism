"Individualism": The social or cultural outlook that emphasizes the individual, their rights, and independence over the collective group or state. 
In the context of international law, the term "para-individualism" refers to:

Attribution of Legal Personality:Corporations are granted a legal personality, allowing them to bear rights and responsibilities in their own name, similar to how an individual person does.
Nationality: This legal personhood is linked to a specific national legal order, which serves as the corporation's "nationality" for international legal purposes, even though a corporation is not a natural person.
Analogical Reasoning: It is an analytical approach that uses the individual as a primary analogy or benchmark for theorizing corporate behavior and regulation in the international legal system. 
This concept helps analyze how multinational corporations are integrated into a legal framework that traditionally assumes individual actors as its primary subjects.
